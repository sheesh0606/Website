Naga is full of spots where it's full of wonderful things you haven't seen yet.

Scroll down to see the majestic famous places that builds Naga to where it is now.

Naga Boardwalk

Photo from Cai Pen

The place is so serene and especially you'll like the view of the ocean. This has no entrance fees, you can freely walk around or sit on the benches provided. The ambience can be very refreshing and relaxing. There are also restaurants nearby so you can always grab snacks or full meal whenever you are hungry.

For commuters, you have two options:

By JEEPNEY: From Colon Street, ride a jeepney with routes 09C/F, or 10F/H and drop off at Jollibee Bulacao or Pardo Church (P8 fare). From there, take another jeepney with route 44 to reach to Naga Plaza (P15 fare). There are also some 44 jeepneys from/near CIT University in N. Bacalso.

By BUS: From South Bus Terminal, ride a bus bound for Bato-Oslob or Carcar. Tell the conductor that you will be hopping off at the Naga Church, which is right along the National Highway.

Pasilong

Photo from Instagram account of France Ian Sinajon @frnsjn

One of the best spots specially when not crowded. It has an amazing and relaxing ambience. The place is well-organized and clean area, also their rest rooms. This would be one of the best spots to hangout here in Cebu. Best day to visit is during weekdays because during weekends expect a very crowded visit.

To get to the Pasilong sa Naga, you have three options:

By Jeepney: From Colon Street, ride a jeepney with routes 09C/F or 10F/H and drop off at Jollibee Bulacao. From there, take the jeepyney with route 44 to reach Naga Boardwalk. There are also #44 jeepneys from CIT University in N. Bacalso.

By Bus: From South Bus Terminal, ride a bus bound for Bato-Oslob or Carcar. Tell the conductor that you will be going down to Naga Church in front of Naga Boardwalk.

By Car: Navigate via Waze or Google / Apple Maps and key in Naga Boardwalk.

Pangilatan Peak

Photo from Wan Dolf

This mountain, while being a lesser-known location, has a certain something special and impressive about it. With its alluring bird's eye views of the city and metro Cebu, this peak in Pangilatan will fascinate you. You can see the ships, the boardwalk, and even the Minglanilla traffic issue vividly from the viewpoint above.

To get there, first you need to take a habal-habal ride to Sitio Pangilatan from Tungkop (Tinong’s Bakery). From there, ask the locals for directions. Since Pangilatan Peak isn’t really known (even to locals), it is suggested that you show a picture of it. In addition, tell the locals that you’re heading to Cantao-an or Mount Magdook. Once you arrive at the crossing, head left, and make your way up to Pangilatan Peak.

Mount Naupa

Photo from Wandering Soul Camper

It's a well-liked and picturesque mountain that has boosted Naga, Cebu, tourism.This Naga, Cebu mountain has been leading the way for many years, enticing hikers and tired city people with its crisp mountain air and stunning vistas. Mount Naupa still makes a great formula for adventure, even though it has gotten a little more popular—especially if you're willing to try new things.

To get there, you have three choices:

BY JEEPNEY: From Cebu City, take a jeepney going Basak. From Basak, take a jeepney with a route Minglanilla-Naga and inform the driver to drop you off the crossing just before the Boys Town in Minglanilla. Look for a habal2x that will take you to the Cogon chapel near the bakery in the crossing.

BY CAR: Just search "Cogon Chapel, Naga, Cebu" in Waze or Google Maps. Parking space is available in front of the Cogon Chapel.

BY BUS: Find your way to the Cebu South Bus Terminal. From there, ride a bus that’s bound for Carcar City and ask the driver to drop you off in Tungkop, Minglanilla. Your landmark is the Sister of Mary School in Minglanilla at the right side of the road. Look for Lolo Tinong’s Bakeshop and you may ride a motorcycle from there going to the drop off area in Brgy. Cogon, just tell your driver you’re going to Mt. Naupa.

Archdiocesan Shrine of St. Francis of Assisi

Photo from Mario Macatol

The parish, which is dedicated to St. Francis of Assisi, features significant historical and cultural value. It attracts visitors not only for its religious importance but also for its architecture and the events it hosts. The church itself is a beautiful structure, often visited by tourists exploring the region. It contributes to the town's cultural and historical tourism appeal.

To get there, you have three choices if you're from Cebu City.

By Car: Take the South Road Properties (SRP) route heading south. Naga is approximately 21 kilometers from Cebu City, and the drive typically takes about 30-45 minutes, depending on traffic.

By Bus: Head to the Cebu South Bus Terminal and board a bus bound for Naga or any destination going further south (like Carcar, Argao, or Dalaguete). Inform the driver or conductor to drop you off at Naga.

By Jeepney: You can also take a jeepney with routes going south from Cebu City. Look for jeepneys with signboards indicating Naga or destinations beyond it.

Parola Seaview Restaurant

Photo from Cebu Car & Van Rental - With Driver

It offers more than just dining. It provides visitors with scenic views of the sea, and creats a memorable experience that attracts both locals and tourists. The combination of good food, picturesque views, and a relaxing ambiance makes it a popular destination for those visiting the area.

To get there, you have two choices if you're from Cebu City.

By Car: Drive south along the Cebu South Road (Natalio B. Bacalso South National Highway). The journey typically takes about an hour, depending on traffic. Look for local signage directing you to the City of Naga. Once you reach the city, follow local directions or use GPS to navigate to Parola Seaview Restaurant.

By Bus: Take a bus from the Cebu South Bus Terminal heading towards the south of Cebu (e.g., buses going to Carcar, Alcoy, or Oslob). Ask the driver to drop you off in Naga City. From there, you may need to take a short tricycle or taxi ride to the restaurant.
